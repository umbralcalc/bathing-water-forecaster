Is it safe to swim?

A calibrated exceedance forecaster for England's designated bathing waters

Each designated bathing water is sampled for E. coli roughly weekly in season. This dashboard ships the fitted model to your browser: drag the antecedent-rainfall and season sliders and watch the probability that the next sample exceeds the threshold move — for every site at once on the map, and broken down into its causes for the site you pick.

How to read this honestly

The model is a censored log-normal on the lab count, driven by antecedent rainfall and a seasonal term. Exceedances are rare (~2–3% of samples) and the truth is observed only weekly, so the forecast's value is calibration — a probability you can trust — and coverage of every designated site, not a large accuracy edge. It is a methodological exercise, not official swimming advice.
