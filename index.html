Bathing water pollution exceedance forecaster

Each designated bathing water site is sampled for E. coli roughly weekly in season. Drag the antecedent-rainfall and season sliders and watch the probability that the next sample exceeds the threshold move for every site at once on the map.

Every probability is produced by one small simulation, fitted to each site's weekly lab history.

This simulation treats the latent E. coli concentration as a censored distribution whose level rises with recent rainfall and shifts through the season, then reads the chance of exceeding the threshold off the simulated distribution.

P(E. coli > 500)
Curve: P(exceed) vs antecedent rainfall at the chosen season. Ticks are this site's recent resolved samples — ▲ exceeded, ▼ within limit.

The model is a censored log-normal on the lab count, driven by antecedent rainfall and a seasonal term. Exceedances are rare (~2-3% of samples) and the truth is observed only weekly.
